# Product

## Register

product

## Users

AI agent builders, CLI-heavy developers, and power users who manage skills, projects, model providers, and usage subscriptions across several coding agents. They use SkillStar during setup and maintenance work, often while switching between local projects, agent tools, and provider accounts.

## Product Purpose

SkillStar centralizes skill discovery, installation, project deployment, AI-assisted reading and translation, provider configuration, and subscription usage visibility for agent workflows. Success means users can keep their skill library organized, understand what each skill does, deploy the right skills to the right agent, and diagnose provider readiness without leaving the desktop app.

## Brand Personality

Precise. Unified. Effortless. The product should feel calm, capable, and technically trustworthy, with enough polish to make complex agent workflows feel manageable rather than ornamental.

## Anti-references

Avoid marketing-site composition inside task surfaces, decorative SaaS card grids, novelty controls for standard actions, and heavy visual effects that slow repeated work. The UI should not look like a generic AI dashboard, a landing page, or a developer tool that hides important state behind vague copy.

## Design Principles

- Show operational state where decisions happen.
- Keep repeated workflows dense, readable, and predictable.
- Preserve user control over automation, providers, and project deployment.
- Make AI assistance explain its readiness, progress, and fallback behavior.
- Prefer stable structure over decorative novelty.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and interactive states. Respect reduced motion, keep progress feedback available to assistive technology, avoid color-only status cues, and ensure keyboard users can reach configuration, translation, and project-deployment controls.
